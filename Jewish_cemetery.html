Jewish Cemetery

The Staryi Sambir Jewish Cemetery is one of the oldest Jewish Cemetery in Ukraine.

It is located near the southern outskirts of the city of Staryi Sambir (Lviv region), on a partially fossil hillside, along the Staryi Sambir - Turka highway.

The cemetery originated in the middle of the XVI century. They hid in it inhabitants of the Staryi Sambir of Jewish nationality. Once in the city there was a fairly large and flourishing Jewish community, whose members were mainly engaged in trade. In the city they had their own stores, small industrial enterprises, and built a synagogue (see the Synagogue of Starosibirsk). The community existed for over 400 years and was destroyed by Nazi invaders during the Second World War.

The cemetery was restored in 1998-2001 on the initiative and at the expense of the birth of the Staryi Sambir Jack Gardner. About 900 tombstones have been restored, some of them are 200-300 years old. The cemetery separated from the road by a low wall. In addition, at the entrance was erected a monument.

The monument and the cemetery are listed in 15 Jewish centers and museums.
